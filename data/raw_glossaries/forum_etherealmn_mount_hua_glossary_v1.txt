# Notes: MTL mistranslations list


Because I'm bored and there's not enough content I decided to list often-showing-up trash MTL translations that doesn't make any sense to help those who want to read the novel. I'm in no way expert in korean, so please correct me if there's something wrong. But feel free to refer to this.



1. Death penalty/Execution/Capital punishment = Sahyung (senior brother, used by disciples within the same rank to call those who enter the sect earlier than them)

2. Living quarters/Living room/Private lodging/Boarding house/Location = Sasuk (senior brother, used by diciples from different rank/distribution to call their senior brother)

3. Accident/Buy = Sago (senior sister)

4. Priest = Saje (same as Sahyung but junior instead of senior)

5. Temper/Sand/Qualification = Sajil (same as Sasuk but junior instead of senior)

6. Brother-in-law/Brother and sister = Samae (junior sister)

7. Seal/Stamp/Painting/Dojo = Dojang (young master, you see it often used by older people to call Chung Myung as he's still very young. Polite and nice way)
(You don't need to translate this term)

8. Long written/Long sentence/Long story/A man of longevity = Jang Mun-In (a way to refer or call the leader of a sect)

9. Ho Gong = The sky, the air/thin air, also the name of a Shaman's elder

10. Horse = depending on the circumstances can mean either Magyo (Heavenly Demon) or "word"

11. Wise ship/Sage ship (현자배/Hyeonja-bae)= referring to the elders (Hyun line)

12. Unja boat (운자배/Unja-bae) = referring to the Un/great disciples, (ex : Unsword = Ungum )

13. White porcelain boat (백자 배/Baekja-bae) = referring to the Baek (2nd class disciples)

14. Celadon belly (청자 배/Cheongja-bae) = referring to the Cheong (3rd class disciples)

15. Magic/Magician/Magistrate = Magyo

16. Congressman/Senator/Lawnmaker = Doctor

17. Saliva = Needle

18. Knife = sometimes refers to sword instead of knife

19. Black = sometimes also refers to sword

20. Bimu = a match, spar, or duel

21. Bongmun = the act of closing the gates and focus on training.
(You don't need to translate this term)

22. Yangmin = ordinary people, civilians
Yangban = martial artists or a person with high position.
They both can simply be translated to "man", but just the class is different
(You don't need to translate these 2 terms)

22. Rain = Bido (비도/throwing knife of the Dang family)

23. Pottery = sometimes can mean Taoist

24. History = Qi or energy

25. Late Index = Referring to an outstanding person among juniors. 후기지수 or 後起之秀 literally means "Rising Star"

26. Herbivore/Province = (초식으론/chosig-eulon)

27. Water lily = Training

28. Katabuta = Word

29. Freeze to death/얼어죽을 = I think you've already get the general idea of what this means by the literal definition alone, but another translation is bullshit/ridiculous/makes no sense

● The translation is messing the viewpoint, so "You" often becomes "I'm", "You" becomes "he", "they" become "I", etc. When reading try to consider the person speaking or the situation and read it in reverse if something doesn't make sense.
● It also messes with pronouns so don't take it too serious when reading
